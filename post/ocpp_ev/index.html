The electric vehicle (EV) revolution is gaining momentum, with more and more drivers making the switch to cleaner transportation. But alongside the growing number of EVs comes the need for a robust and standardized charging infrastructure. This is where the Open Charge Point Protocol (OCPP) comes in. In this blog post, we’ll explore what OCPP is, why it’s essential, and how it works.

What is OCPP? Imagine a world where you can pull up to any EV charging station, regardless of brand, and seamlessly connect your car to charge. That’s the potential of OCPP. Developed by the Open Charge Alliance (OCA), OCPP is an open-source communication protocol that acts as the universal language between EV charging stations and charging network management systems (CSMS).

Refer: https://openchargealliance.org/

OCPP has undergone several iterations since its inception, with each version introducing new features and improvements. The most widely used versions include OCPP 1.5, 1.6, and the latest OCPP 2.0. Each version addresses the evolving needs of the EV charging industry, such as enhanced security, improved interoperability, and support for smart grid integration.

Building blocks of the eco system:

  • EV Charging Station: Think of it as the hardware you plug your car into.
  • CSMS: This is the software platform that manages the network of charging stations, tracks energy usage, and facilitates payments.

OCPP ensures these two components can talk to each other, enabling features like:

  • Initiating and stopping charging sessions
  • Monitoring charging status and energy consumption
  • Remote diagnostics and maintenance
  • Authorization and billing

Benefits of OCPP:

  • Standardization: OCPP promotes interoperability, allowing drivers to use any OCPP-compliant charger with any compatible network.
  • Flexibility: Network operators can choose from a variety of charger and software vendors without worrying about compatibility issues.
  • Innovation: OCPP fosters innovation within the EV charging industry by creating a level playing field for different players.
  • Efficiency: Standardized communication simplifies network management and reduces operational costs.

Implementing OCPP:

The specification is available at https://openchargealliance.org/my-oca/ocpp/ Major used versions of OCPP are OPEN CHARGE POINT PROTOCOL 1.6 and OPEN CHARGE POINT PROTOCOL 2.0.1

There are not much open source OCPP implementation libraries available.

One popular open source implementtaion available is ChargeTime/Java-OCA-OCPP (https://github.com/ChargeTimeEU/Java-OCA-OCPP) There aren’t many widely used Java libraries specifically for OCPP besides ChargeTime. You might find some less popular options on platforms like GitHub. Thoroughly evaluate their documentation, community support, and activity level before using them in a critical project. We will deep dive on how to use this library in our next blog post.

It is better to develop a custom OCPP implementation yourselves if you have a broader time frame to take your product to market. This approach gives you complete control over the OCPP communication layer. It requires a deep understanding of the OCPP protocol and can be time-consuming to develop and maintain.

The Future of OCPP:

As the EV landscape continues to evolve, OCPP is expected to play an even more critical role. Newer versions of the protocol are being developed to address future needs, such as:

  • Smart charging: Optimizing charging based on grid conditions and user preferences.
  • Plug-and-charge: Seamless charging experience without any authentication required.
  • Vehicle-to-grid integration: Enabling EVs to interact with the power grid and provide grid stability services.
